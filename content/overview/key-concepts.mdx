## Core XACML Concepts

Understanding these building blocks is essential before creating policies.

<InfoCard title="Subject" variant="teal">
The entity requesting access — typically a user or service. Identified by attributes like role, department, or clearance level.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Resource" variant="teal">
What is being accessed — an API endpoint, data entity, or business function. Identified by type, ID, or classification.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Action" variant="teal">
The operation being performed — read, write, delete, approve. Maps to HTTP methods or business operations.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Environment" variant="teal">
Contextual conditions — time of day, IP address, location, risk score. Enables context-aware authorization.
</InfoCard>

## Policy Structure

<InfoCard title="Policy" variant="purple">
A set of rules that define access control logic. Each policy targets specific subjects, resources, and actions.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Rule" variant="purple">
The smallest unit of authorization logic. Evaluates to Permit, Deny, or NotApplicable based on conditions.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Combining Algorithm" variant="purple">
Determines the overall decision when multiple rules or policies apply. Common algorithms: first-applicable, deny-overrides, permit-overrides.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Obligation" variant="purple">
Actions that MUST be performed alongside the authorization decision — like logging, notifications, or additional checks.
</InfoCard>
